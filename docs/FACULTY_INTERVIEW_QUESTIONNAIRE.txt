================================================================================
  ENROLLPRO SYSTEM — FACULTY INTERVIEW QUESTIONNAIRE
  Web-Based School Admission, Enrollment & Information Management System
================================================================================

  Purpose     : Gather faculty feedback on system features within scope
  Prepared by : Systems Analyst
  Date        : April 1, 2026
  System Scope: Five modules only —
                  1. Admission (Online + Face-to-Face Walk-in)
                  2. Enrollment Management
                  3. Student Information Management System (SIMS)
                  4. Teacher Management
                  5. Grade Level & Sectioning Management
  Format      : Structured by User Role, grouped by focus area
  Instruction : Use a 5-point Likert scale (1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly
                Agree) for rating questions. Record verbatim responses for
                open-ended items. Allow follow-up probing as needed.

================================================================================
                     SECTION A — DEPARTMENT HEADS
================================================================================

  Context: Department Heads coordinate SCP (Special Curricular Programs) like
  STE, SPA, SPS, SPJ, SPFL, and SPTVE within their departments and rely on
  section rosters and student classification data produced by the system.

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  A1. POLICY ALIGNMENT
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  A1.1  The system collects Sensitive Personal Information (SPI) — disability
        status, IP community affiliation, and 4Ps beneficiary status — as
        part of the BEEF (Basic Education Enrollment Form). Under RA 10173,
        these fields are restricted to Registrar and System Admin roles only
        and are never shown in directory listings.

        (a) In your department, who currently handles student SPI data
            (disability, IP, 4Ps), and through what means (physical
            folders, spreadsheets, verbal handoff from the Registrar)?

        (b) For your department's SCP programs, are there SPI-adjacent
            data points that factor into admission decisions (e.g., IP
            affiliation for IPEd programs)? How are they currently
            collected?

        (c) The system records a timestamped RA 10173 consent for every
            applicant (online: scroll-to-read + checkbox; walk-in:
            Registrar confirms physical signature). Is your department
            currently involved in any part of the consent process?

  A1.2  The system's SIMS stores digitized SF8 health records (weight,
        height, BMI computed at display time, nutritional status) for
        each learner at BoSY and EoSY assessment points. The Registrar
        enters this data on behalf of the class adviser or school nurse.

        (a) Does your department currently use SF8 health data for any
            program-level decisions (e.g., feeding program referrals,
            MAPEH/PE medical clearance)?

        (b) If the system automatically classifies nutritional status
            (Severely Wasted, Wasted, Normal, Overweight, Obese) from
            weight and height, would your department heads reference
            this data for student welfare responses?

  A1.3  Every action in the system — application status changes, student
        record edits, enrollment decisions — is captured in an immutable
        audit log with timestamp and actor identity. No user can delete
        audit entries.

        (a) How does your department currently track accountability for
            enrollment-related decisions (e.g., SCP admission results,
            section placement recommendations)?

        (b) Would read access to the audit trail for SCP assessment
            decisions improve transparency within your department?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  A2. WORKFLOW INTEGRATION
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  A2.1  SCP Assessment Workflow — the system tracks each SCP applicant
        through a structured pipeline:
          PENDING → EXAM_SCHEDULED → EXAM_TAKEN → PASSED/FAILED → APPROVED

        (a) Describe your department's current process for scheduling
            and administering SCP entrance exams or auditions. Who
            decides the exam date, venue, and cutoff score?

        (b) After assessments are conducted, how are results currently
            recorded and communicated to the Registrar for applicant
            status updates?

        (c) The system allows the Registrar to record exam scores,
            audition results, and interview notes per applicant. Would
            your department prefer to provide these results directly
            in the system, or submit them to the Registrar for entry?

        (d) The system sends automated email notifications to applicants
            when their exam is scheduled and when results are released.
            Would this reduce the communication work your department
            currently handles for SCP admissions?

  A2.2  Section Roster Availability — the system generates section
        assignments as soon as the Registrar approves and assigns
        applicants during Phase 1 (Early Registration) and finalizes
        them during Phase 2 (Regular Enrollment).

        (a) How many days or weeks before class opening does your
            department currently receive finalized section rosters?

        (b) If section rosters were available digitally in the system
            immediately after assignment, would this improve your
            department's preparation timeline?

        (c) When a student transfers mid-year and the Registrar updates
            the SIMS record, how would real-time roster updates affect
            your department's tracking of students per section?

  A2.3  Teacher-to-Section Assignment — the system's Teacher Management
        module stores teacher profiles and their section advisory
        assignments.

        (a) How does your department currently receive official teacher-
            to-section advisory assignments each school year?

        (b) If the system displayed each section's adviser alongside
            student count and capacity, would that help your department
            verify load distribution?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  A3. PAIN POINTS & TECHNICAL GAPS
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  A3.1  The system provides a public application tracker at
        /track/:trackingNumber where parents check status without login.

        (a) During Early Registration, does your department receive
            direct inquiries from parents about SCP admission status?
            Estimate the volume (calls, walk-ins) per week.

        (b) If parents could self-check application status online,
            what percentage of those inquiries would be eliminated?

  A3.2  Gaps between current manual processes and the digital system:

        (a) List the top 3 bottlenecks in your department related to
            enrollment, student intake, or SCP admission processing.

        (b) Are there any department-level records, logbooks, or
            spreadsheets related to admission, enrollment, student
            records, teacher assignments, or sectioning that are NOT
            covered by the system's five modules?

  A3.3  Device and connectivity:

        (a) What devices does your department use for administrative
            work (desktop, laptop, tablet, phone)?

        (b) The system is fully responsive (functional at 320px screen
            width). Would mobile access during enrollment period be
            useful for your department?

        (c) Is the school's internet connection reliable during peak
            enrollment periods (January–February for Phase 1, June
            for Phase 2)?


================================================================================
                     SECTION B — TEACHERS (Class Advisers & Subject Teachers)
================================================================================

  Context: Teachers are affected by the system's outputs — section rosters,
  student records in SIMS, and SF8 health data entry. Teacher profiles are
  managed in the Teacher Management module. Teachers do not have system
  login accounts by default.

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  B1. POLICY ALIGNMENT
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  B1.1  Student privacy — the system restricts SPI data (4Ps, IP,
        disability) to Registrar and System Admin roles only.

        (a) How do you currently receive your class list at the start
            of the school year — printed roster, email, verbal from
            the Registrar?

        (b) Does your current class list include any SPI indicators
            (disability, 4Ps, IP)? If yes, how is that information
            communicated to you?

        (c) Should teachers have read-only access to student profiles
            in the system (excluding SPI data), or should access remain
            exclusively with the Registrar/Admin? Why?

  B1.2  Learner Self-Service Portal — the system allows students and
        parents to view their own records at /learner using LRN + Date
        of Birth (no login account required, read-only access).

        (a) In your experience, do parents currently ask you for student
            enrollment status or personal record information? How often?

        (b) If students could view their own profile (demographics,
            enrollment history, health records) through the self-service
            portal, would this reduce information requests directed at
            you?

        (c) Do you see any concerns with students accessing records
            using LRN + Date of Birth (e.g., if LRNs are known among
            classmates)?

  B1.3  SF8 Health Records — as a class adviser, you conduct BoSY and
        EoSY measurements (weight, height) per DepEd Order No. 13, s.
        2017. The system digitizes these at the individual learner level.

        (a) Describe your current SF8 workflow: who measures, who
            records, and in what format (paper SF8, Excel, other)?

        (b) How long does it take you to complete SF8 measurements
            and recording for your entire advisory section?

        (c) The Registrar enters your submitted measurements into the
            system (you provide paper/spreadsheet data; the Registrar
            digitizes it). Is this acceptable, or would you prefer
            direct data entry access?

        (d) The system auto-computes BMI, BMI-for-age classification,
            and Height-for-Age from the weight and height values you
            provide. Do you currently calculate these manually or use
            a separate DepEd BMI tool?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  B2. WORKFLOW INTEGRATION
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  B2.1  Section roster and student information:

        (a) How many days before class opening do you currently receive
            your finalized class list and section advisory assignment?

        (b) What minimum student information do you need before the
            first day of class (name, LRN, special program enrollment,
            medical conditions)?

        (c) The system makes section rosters available digitally as
            soon as Phase 2 enrollment is completed. If you could view
            them on any device, how would this affect your preparation?

  B2.2  Mid-year student movement:

        (a) How are you currently notified when a student transfers
            into or out of your section mid-year?

        (b) Have you experienced issues with outdated class lists after
            mid-year transfers? Describe.

        (c) If the system's SIMS updated your section roster in real
            time once the Registrar processes a transfer, how useful
            would that be? (Rate 1–5)

  B2.3  Teacher Management module — the system stores your profile
        (name, contact, advisory assignment, subject load) managed by
        the Registrar.

        (a) How is your teacher profile information currently collected
            — through a form you fill out yearly, verbal confirmation,
            or carried forward from last year?

        (b) If your profile and section assignment were visible in the
            system, would you want the ability to verify or flag errors
            in your own record?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  B3. PAIN POINTS & TECHNICAL GAPS
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  B3.1  Data re-entry and duplication:

        (a) How many times per school year do you re-enter student
            demographic or enrollment data across different school
            forms (SF1, SF2, SF8)?

        (b) If the system's SIMS already contains all BEEF demographics,
            enrollment history, and health records, which of your manual
            forms could be partially pre-populated from system data?

  B3.2  The student profile in SIMS has 5 tabs: Personal Information,
        Academic History, Application Record, Classifications & Special
        Programs, and Health Records.

        (a) Which of these tabs contain information most relevant to
            your daily work as a class adviser or subject teacher?

        (b) What student information do you currently lack that you
            wish you had at the start of the school year?

  B3.3  Technology readiness:

        (a) Rate your comfort level with web-based systems (1 = not
            comfortable, 5 = very comfortable).

        (b) What training format would you prefer for learning the
            system (hands-on workshop, video tutorials, printed guide,
            one-on-one coaching)?

        (c) What is your primary concern about transitioning from
            manual enrollment-related processes to a digital system?


================================================================================
                SECTION C — SCHOOL ADMINISTRATORS (Principal & Asst. Principal)
================================================================================

  Context: School Administrators provide oversight for the enrollment process,
  manage staffing, and ensure DepEd policy compliance. In EnrollPro, they may
  hold the SYSTEM_ADMIN role (which inherits all Registrar capabilities plus
  user management, email logs, and system health monitoring).

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  C1. POLICY ALIGNMENT
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  C1.1  RA 10173 — Data Privacy governance:

        (a) Who is currently designated as the school's Data Privacy
            Officer or Data Protection focal person?

        (b) The system enforces consent collection before any data
            entry: online applicants must scroll through and accept
            the RA 10173 notice; walk-in applicants have a physical
            consent form acknowledged by the Registrar. Does your
            school currently collect written DP consent during
            enrollment?

        (c) SPI data (4Ps, IP, disability) is restricted to Registrar
            and System Admin roles — never shown in student directory
            listings, only in individual profiles. Should any additional
            staff roles have access to SPI data? Under what conditions?

        (d) The learner self-service portal uses LRN + Date of Birth
            for read-only access (no password needed). Do you see any
            risk with this approach at your school?

  C1.2  Audit and accountability:

        (a) The system's audit log is immutable — no user, including
            the System Admin, can delete entries. Every application
            status change, student record edit, user account action,
            and enrollment gate toggle is logged. Does this align with
            compliance expectations from your SDO or regional office?

        (b) The Registrar sees audit logs for their own actions and
            system events. The System Admin sees all users' audit logs.
            Is this visibility split appropriate for your school?

  C1.3  Two-phase enrollment alignment:

        (a) The system follows DepEd Order No. 017, s. 2025 — Phase 1
            (Early Registration, last Saturday of January to last
            Friday of February) for Grade 7, Grade 11, transferees,
            and Balik-Aral; Phase 2 (Regular Enrollment, ~1 week
            before June class opening) for all grade levels.

            Are there division-level policies or SDO memoranda at your
            school that modify these standard dates or add additional
            requirements to either phase?

        (b) The system auto-calculates Phase 1 and Phase 2 dates from
            the academic year label using DepEd's fixed calendar rules.
            Are there cases where your school needs to override these
            computed dates (e.g., SDO-granted extensions)?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  C2. WORKFLOW INTEGRATION
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  C2.1  Academic Year lifecycle — the system manages the full cycle:
          Create AY → Configure grade levels, strands, SCP programs →
          Build sections → Assign teachers → Activate AY → Open
          enrollment gate → Process applications → Enroll → Archive

        (a) Who in your school currently decides when the enrollment
            gate (online application portal) opens and closes — the
            principal, the registrar, or the SDO?

        (b) How many staff members would need system accounts?
            Estimate by role (registrar, department head, system admin, other).

        (c) The system enforces password change on first login and
            tracks last login timestamps. Does your school have a
            policy on password rotation or session management?

        (d) If a staff member leaves mid-year, the System Admin
            deactivates their account (soft delete — data and audit
            trail are preserved). Is this consistent with your
            offboarding process?

  C2.2  Dashboard and enrollment monitoring:

        (a) The dashboard shows: total enrolled count, pending
            applications, approved-awaiting-enrollment count, sections
            at capacity, enrollment by grade level (bar chart), status
            distribution (donut chart), and online vs. walk-in channel
            breakdown.

            Which of these metrics do you currently monitor, and how
            (manual count, spreadsheet, reports from the Registrar)?

        (b) How frequently do you need enrollment snapshot data during
            the enrollment period (daily, weekly, on-demand)?

        (c) Are there enrollment-related metrics not listed above that
            you need for internal or SDO reporting?

  C2.3  Section and teacher configuration:

        (a) How are sections created each year at your school —
            carried over from the previous year, rebuilt from scratch,
            or a mix?

        (b) The system enforces section capacity limits and displays a
            real-time capacity monitor. How do you currently track
            section sizes, and what happens when a section is full?

        (c) The Teacher Management module stores teacher profiles and
            section advisory assignments. Are there teacher data points
            beyond name, contact, and advisory assignment that the
            system should capture for your administrative needs?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  C3. PAIN POINTS & TECHNICAL GAPS
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  C3.1  Current enrollment process:

        (a) Rate the efficiency of your current enrollment process
            on a scale of 1–5 (1 = very inefficient, 5 = very
            efficient).

        (b) What is the single biggest source of delay or error in
            your current enrollment cycle?

        (c) Estimate the average time (in minutes) for one walk-in
            applicant from arrival to completion. What would be an
            acceptable target with the digital system?

  C3.2  System deployment:

        (a) Does the school have a dedicated IT staff or ICT
            coordinator who would serve as the System Administrator?

        (b) What is the school's internet infrastructure (connection
            type, speed, reliability during peak enrollment hours)?

        (c) Is there a preferred deployment window (e.g., April–May
            summer break) that minimizes disruption?

        (d) Rank these four factors by importance for successful
            adoption: ease of use, processing speed, data accuracy,
            DepEd policy compliance.

  C3.3  Change management:

        (a) What resistance to digital adoption have you observed or
            anticipated from staff?

        (b) Would a phased rollout (e.g., online admission module
            first, then SIMS, then full enrollment management) be
            preferred over a full system launch?

        (c) Who should be the primary "system champion" at your school
            responsible for driving adoption and collecting feedback?


================================================================================
                  SECTION D — REGISTRAR / ENROLLMENT OFFICER
================================================================================

  Context: The Registrar is the primary daily operator of EnrollPro —
  processing all applications (online and walk-in), managing enrollments,
  maintaining SIMS records, entering SF8 health data, configuring academic
  years, managing sections, and managing teacher records.

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  D1. POLICY ALIGNMENT
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  D1.1  RA 10173 compliance in daily operations:

        (a) How do you currently collect and store RA 10173 consent
            during enrollment — written forms? Where are they filed?

        (b) The system logs a timestamped consent record with version
            tracking (e.g., "RA10173-v2025-DO017") for every online
            applicant. For walk-ins, you check a box confirming the
            physical consent was signed. Is this sufficient for your
            SDO's requirements?

        (c) When parents request access to, correction of, or deletion
            of their child's data (RA 10173 data subject rights), how
            do you currently handle these requests?

  D1.2  Document verification during admission:

        (a) The system requires PSA Birth Certificate, SF9/Report Card,
            and the BEEF form. For SCP applicants, additional documents
            may apply. Are there documents your school requires beyond
            this list?

        (b) The system supports two admission channels — Online (public
            portal, gated by enrollment toggle) and F2F walk-in (always
            available to authenticated staff). What is the approximate
            ratio of online vs. walk-in applicants at your school?

        (c) For walk-in applicants, the system allows an immediate
            APPROVED status if all documents are verified on the spot.
            Is this consistent with your current practice, or do all
            applicants go through a review period?

  D1.3  Application status management — the system uses a defined
        status pipeline:
          Regular: PENDING → APPROVED → ENROLLED (or REJECTED)
          SCP:     PENDING → EXAM_SCHEDULED → EXAM_TAKEN →
                   PASSED/FAILED → APPROVED → ENROLLED (or REJECTED)

        (a) Does this status flow match your current processing steps?
            Are there intermediate states you currently use that are
            not listed?

        (b) APPROVED means Phase 1 pre-registration complete (slot
            reserved + section assigned). ENROLLED means Phase 2
            finalized (official enrollment of record). Is this
            two-status distinction clear and workable for your office?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  D2. WORKFLOW INTEGRATION
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  D2.1  Daily application processing:

        (a) During peak Early Registration (January–February), how
            many applications do you process per day (combined online
            and walk-in)?

        (b) Describe your step-by-step process for one walk-in
            applicant, from the moment they enter the Registrar's
            office to the moment they leave.

        (c) The system provides a three-level review flow: application
            list with filters → slide-over quick review panel → full
            detail page for complex cases → action dialogs (approve,
            reject, schedule exam). Would this multi-level approach
            improve or complicate your current workflow?

  D2.2  SCP assessment coordination:

        (a) For SCP programs (STE, SPA, SPS, SPJ, SPFL, SPTVE), who
            currently schedules entrance exams — you, the department
            head, or the SDO?

        (b) How are exam results currently communicated to applicants
            (posted list, text message, phone call)?

        (c) The system sends automated email notifications at each
            SCP status change (exam scheduled, passed, failed). Is
            email a reliable channel for your parent community?

  D2.3  Phase 2 transition — moving applicants from APPROVED to ENROLLED:

        (a) When Phase 2 opens in June, what additional steps do you
            perform on pre-registered (APPROVED) students before
            officially enrolling them?

        (b) How do you currently assign sections to incoming Grade 7
            and Grade 11 students — by class size, by program, by
            alphabetical order, by adviser preference?

        (c) The system displays section capacity in real time and
            prevents over-enrollment. How do you currently monitor
            section fill rates?

  D2.4  SIMS — Student Information Management:

        (a) How do you currently maintain student records across
            multiple school years — physical folders, spreadsheets,
            a combination?

        (b) The system's SIMS provides a searchable directory
            (filter by grade, section, year, status, channel) and
            a 5-tab student profile (Personal Info, Academic History,
            Application Record, Classifications, Health Records).
            Which of these features would have the most impact on
            your daily work?

        (c) How do you currently handle requests for student records
            from other schools (transferee credentials)?

  D2.5  Settings and configuration:

        (a) The Settings page has 4 tabs: School Profile (logo, name,
            division), Academic Year (create/activate/archive), Grade
            Levels & Strands & SCP Programs, and Enrollment Gate
            (open/close portal). How often do these settings change
            within a single school year?

        (b) Who decides when to open the online admission portal and
            when to close it?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  D3. PAIN POINTS & TECHNICAL GAPS
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  D3.1  Pain point ranking — rate each by severity (1 = minor, 5 = severe):

        [ ] Duplicate data entry across multiple forms/systems
        [ ] Lost or misfiled physical application forms
        [ ] Difficulty tracking application status over days/weeks
        [ ] Parent/guardian inquiries consuming work time
        [ ] Generating enrollment statistics manually
        [ ] Section capacity miscounts or over-enrollment
        [ ] Coordinating SCP exam scheduling and results
        [ ] Maintaining student records across school years
        [ ] Other: _______________________________________________

  D3.2  System-specific concerns:

        (a) The system uses email (via SMTP) for automated applicant
            notifications. Do most applicant families at your school
            have active email addresses? If not, what is the most
            effective communication channel (SMS, social media, posted
            physical list)?

        (b) The public application tracker (/track/:trackingNumber)
            requires only the tracking number — no login. Is this
            sufficient, or would you prefer additional verification
            (e.g., tracking number + date of birth)?

        (c) If internet connectivity is lost during an enrollment day,
            what is your current fallback process?

  D3.3  Training and transition:

        (a) How many staff in the Registrar's office would use the
            system daily?

        (b) What is the best time to conduct system training without
            disrupting enrollment operations?

        (c) During transition, would you need to run the manual process
            in parallel with the digital system? If so, for how long?

                         SECTION E — GUIDANCE COUNSELOR
================================================================================

  Context: Guidance Counselors interact with student classification data,
  SCP placement outcomes, and learner welfare information managed by the
  system. They are not primary system operators but are downstream consumers
  of enrollment and student record data.

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  E1. POLICY ALIGNMENT
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  E1.1  The system classifies learners by type (New Enrollee, Transferee,
        Balik-Aral) and tracks SPI indicators (4Ps, IP, LWD). SPI is
        restricted to Registrar/Admin roles only.

        (a) Do you currently receive SPI data (disability, IP, 4Ps) for
            incoming students? Through what channel and how timely?

        (b) Should the Guidance Counselor role have read-only access to
            SPI data in the system for intervention planning? What
            safeguards would be appropriate?

  E1.2  The system tracks SCP assessment outcomes (exam scores, audition
        results, interview notes) in each applicant's record.

        (a) Are you involved in SCP admission interviews or placement
            decisions? Describe your role if any.

        (b) When a student is classified NOT_QUALIFIED for an SCP program,
            who handles the counseling conversation and potential
            reclassification to a regular section? How is this currently
            coordinated with the Registrar?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  E2. WORKFLOW INTEGRATION
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  E2.1  The system's SIMS student profile includes: Personal Information,
        Academic History (all enrollment records across school years),
        Application Record (original submission + status timeline),
        Classifications & Special Programs, and Health Records (SF8).

        (a) When do you currently first receive information about
            incoming students relative to class opening?

        (b) Which of the 5 SIMS profile tabs would be most valuable
            for early identification of at-risk students?

        (c) If the system provided you a filtered student view
            (demographics, learner type, classifications, health
            summary — excluding SPI unless authorized), would this
            improve your case management?

  E2.2  Mid-year student movement:

        (a) When a student transfers in mid-year, how do you currently
            receive their background information from the Registrar?

        (b) If the system's SIMS made transfer records immediately
            accessible (enrollment history, previous school, learner
            type), how would this affect your response time for new
            transfer students?

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  E3. PAIN POINTS
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  E3.1  (a) What is the biggest information gap you face when new
            students are assigned to the school at the start of the
            year?

        (b) How many days or weeks into the school year does it take
            before you have complete background information on all
            incoming students?

        (c) Are there any student welfare data points within the scope
            of admission and enrollment (not grades, not attendance)
            that you collect but have no system to store them in?
